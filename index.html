Explore Dog Breeds

Labrador Retriever

History: Originating in Newfoundland, Canada, Labradors were bred to assist fishermen in retrieving nets and fish. They became popular in the UK and North America.

Food Habits: Active dogs needing high-quality protein. Prone to obesity, so portion control is key. Benefit from joint-supporting supplements.

German Shepherd

History: Developed in Germany in the late 19th century for herding sheep, they quickly gained recognition for their intelligence and versatility.

Food Habits: Require a diet supporting their active lifestyle and muscle mass. Some are sensitive to certain grains; consider grain-free options if needed.

Golden Retriever

History: Originated in Scotland in the mid-19th century, bred to retrieve game from both land and water during hunting parties.

Food Habits: Prone to allergies and hip/elbow dysplasia. A balanced diet with omega fatty acids and controlled portions is beneficial.

French Bulldog

History: Descended from English Bulldogs, they were miniaturized in England and became popular in France among lace workers in the 1800s.

Food Habits: Prone to digestive issues and allergies. A high-quality, easily digestible diet is recommended. Avoid overfeeding to prevent obesity.

Poodle

History: Despite their association with France, Poodles originated in Germany as water retrievers. Their distinctive coat clips were functional for swimming.

Food Habits: Can be prone to sensitive stomachs and allergies. A balanced diet with quality ingredients and good fats for coat health is ideal.

Beagle

History: Small hounds used for hunting hare in England for centuries. Their keen sense of smell makes them excellent scent detectors.

Food Habits: Known for their love of food and tendency to gain weight. Strict portion control and a balanced diet are crucial.

Bulldog

History: Originated in England, bred for bull-baiting. After the sport was outlawed, they were refined into the gentle companions we know today.

Food Habits: Prone to allergies and sensitive digestion. A high-quality, limited-ingredient diet can be beneficial. Avoid overfeeding due to lower activity levels.

Rottweiler

History: Descended from Roman drover dogs, they were used to herd livestock and pull carts in Germany. Named after the town Rottweil.

Food Habits: Large, muscular dogs needing a diet rich in protein and nutrients for joint health. Prone to bloat, so smaller, frequent meals are advised.

Dachshund

History: German in origin, bred to hunt badgers and other burrow-dwelling animals. Their long, low bodies were ideal for going into tunnels.

Food Habits: Prone to back problems, so maintaining a healthy weight is critical. High-quality dog food in controlled portions is recommended.

Siberian Husky

History: Originated in Northeast Asia by the Chukchi people for pulling sleds long distances in harsh conditions. Known for their endurance.

Food Habits: High energy dogs needing a diet to support their activity. Can be prone to zinc deficiency; a balanced, high-quality diet is important.

Border Collie

History: Developed in the Anglo-Scottish border region for herding sheep. Renowned for their intelligence, intense stare, and working ability.

Food Habits: Highly active dogs requiring a diet rich in protein and healthy fats to sustain their energy levels. Avoid overfeeding to prevent weight gain.

Australian Shepherd

History: Despite the name, they developed in the Western U.S. during the 19th century, used by Basque shepherds. Known for agility and herding.

Food Habits: Energetic dogs needing a high-quality diet to support their active lifestyle. Can be prone to certain sensitivities, so monitor their reaction to food.
